# Brand Identity

> Version: 2.0 — Aligned with 01_Vision.md and 02_Product_Principles.md

## Brand Vision

Build the most premium outlet marketplace in Europe.

The brand should feel like a modern technology company rather than a discount marketplace.

The brand's job in one sentence:
**make a discounted product feel like a smart decision, never like a compromise.**

# Tone of Voice

Use short, confident sentences.

Avoid marketing buzzwords.

Write like Apple and Airbnb.

Example:

Bad:
"Huge unbelievable discounts!!!"

Good:
"Premium products. Better prices."

## How we talk about flaws

Flaws are stated calmly, specifically, and first-person honest.

Bad:
"Gently handled, almost like new!"

Good:
"Three months on our showroom floor. A faint scratch on the left side — photographed below."

Honesty about flaws IS the brand. A flaw described plainly builds more trust than a flaw hidden well.

## Lexicon — words we use and words we ban

| We say | We never say |
|---|---|
| Display item | Used |
| Grade B | Minor issues |
| Why it's discounted | Special deal!!! |
| 1 unit available | HURRY! Almost gone! |
| Verified business | Trusted seller™ |
| You save €550 | %90 SHOCK DISCOUNT |

No exclamation marks in selling copy. Ever.
Numbers do the convincing; the voice stays calm.

Primary:
Black / White

Secondary:
Neutral grays

Accent:
Emerald (success, verification)
Amber (warning)
Red (errors only — **never for prices or discounts**)

## Price & Discount Display

The discount is our core content, so it gets a deliberate, elegant, consistent treatment:

- Original price: muted gray, strikethrough.
- Atlas price: large, black (or white on dark), the visual anchor.
- Savings: calm percentage or amount ("–42%" or "You save €550") in a neutral or emerald tone.
- Never red discount tags, never starburst badges, never "SALE" stickers.

All color pairings must meet WCAG AA contrast. Elegance never excuses illegibility.

# Photography

Photography is the product.

Rules:
- Bright lighting
- Real images only
- Neutral backgrounds
- Multiple angles
- Detail shots for defects — shot with the same care as beauty shots. We photograph flaws honestly AND beautifully; a well-lit, sharp flaw photo says "we have nothing to hide".
- No stock photos as primary imagery
- Flaw photos are labeled ("Flaw 1 of 3") and never buried at the end of the gallery.

# Condition & Grade Badges

## Condition badges (the story — matches 01_Vision.md exactly)

- Factory New
- Open Box
- Display Item
- Excess Inventory
- Returned Product
- Demo Unit
- Trade Show Item
- Refurbished
- Cosmetic Damage
- Packaging Damage

One vocabulary everywhere: badges, filters, emails, invoices use these exact terms. No synonyms, no local variants.

## Grade badges (the state)

Grade A / Grade B / Grade C is a first-class brand asset.
Goal: "Atlas Grade B" becomes a term buyers trust and repeat.

- Monochrome, typographic, calm — a letter in a simple shape, not a colorful sticker.
- Same size and position on every product card and listing.
- Never decorated, never animated, never colored by "severity" — Grade C is honest information, not a warning to be painted amber.

Badge styling is defined once in the design system and reused everywhere.

# Trust Signals

Visual marks of the Trust Engine (01_Vision.md):

- Verified Business — emerald check, next to the seller name.
- Atlas Grade — the A/B/C badge.
- Transparency Card — its consistent placement IS a trust signal; buyers learn to look for it.
- Secure Payment / escrow note at checkout.
- 14-Day Returns.
- "Flaws photographed" indicator on Grade B/C listings.

Trust signals are quiet and consistent — they earn belief through repetition, not size.

---

# Naming Note

"Atlas" is a codename. The final name must:

- Work in Turkish first (launch market) AND across European languages (no unfortunate meanings anywhere).
- Be pronounceable and spellable from hearing it once.
- Have a clean domain and trademark path.
- Not contain "outlet", "discount" or "deal" — the name should signal premium, the products signal the price.
