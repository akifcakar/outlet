## Vision

Atlas is the marketplace where premium products get a second chance.

Our mission is to become the most trusted outlet marketplace in Europe by transforming display items, open-box products, excess inventory and showroom products into discoverable, desirable purchases.

## Problem

### The Seller's Problem

Retailers constantly struggle with products that cannot be sold through their regular sales channels:

- Display products
- Open-box products
- Cancelled orders
- Returned items
- Overstock inventory
- End-of-season inventory
- Trade show products
- Slightly damaged products
- Packaging damaged products

Most of these products are perfectly usable. Yet they sit in warehouses, tie up capital, and eventually get liquidated for a fraction of their value — or thrown away.

Current solutions are fragmented and unprofessional:
- Instagram Stories
- WhatsApp groups
- Outlet corners
- Random marketplace listings

**The hidden problem: channel conflict.**
Brands and retailers fear that publicly discounting products damages their main sales channel and brand perception. This is the real reason outlet inventory is sold quietly through WhatsApp groups. Atlas must solve this fear, not just provide listings.

### The Buyer's Problem

Buyers who want premium products at outlet prices face:

- **No trust:** Is this really new? Why is it discounted? Is the seller real?
- **No standards:** "Lightly used", "like new", "minor scratch" mean different things to every seller.
- **No discovery:** Deals are hidden in closed groups and stories that disappear in 24 hours.
- **No protection:** Payment by bank transfer to a stranger, no returns, no recourse.

## The Trust Engine

Trust is Atlas's core product. It is built from four layers:

### 1. Verified Sellers
- Only registered businesses (tax ID / company registration verified).
- Seller identity, location, and track record visible on every listing.
- Seller performance score (shipping speed, accuracy of condition, dispute rate).
- Three strikes: repeated misgraded listings → removal from the platform.

### 2. Standardized Condition Grading
The condition label answers "what happened to this product".
The grade answers "what state is it in now". Both are mandatory.

**Condition (the story):**
- Factory New
- Open Box
- Display Item
- Excess Inventory
- Returned Product
- Demo Unit
- Trade Show Item
- Refurbished
- Cosmetic Damage
- Packaging Damage

**Grade (the state):**
- **Grade A** — No visible flaws. Indistinguishable from new.
- **Grade B** — Minor cosmetic marks, visible only on close inspection. Fully functional.
- **Grade C** — Clear cosmetic wear or repaired damage. Fully functional. Every flaw photographed.

Every Grade B and C listing must include close-up photos of every flaw.

### 3. The Transparency Card
Every listing carries a standard card, always in the same place, always in the same format:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Condition | Display Item |
| Grade | B |
| Why discounted | Floor model at our İstanbul showroom for 3 months |
| Original price | ₺12.999 |
| Atlas price | ₺7.499 (–42%) |
| Warranty | 12 months seller warranty |
| Return policy | 14-day return |
| Stock | 1 unit |

This card is the heart of the product. It converts anxiety into confidence.

### 4. Buyer Protection
- Payment held in escrow until delivery is confirmed.
- 14-day return right (aligned with Turkish distance-sales consumer law; EU-compatible for later expansion).
- "Not as described" guarantee: if the product doesn't match its grade, return shipping is free and the seller is penalized.
- Structured dispute resolution — no arguing in comment sections.

## Solving Channel Conflict (Why Sellers Will Come)

Sellers don't just need a sales channel — they need a *safe* sales channel. Atlas offers:

- **A separate audience:** Atlas buyers are deal-seekers, a different segment from full-price buyers. Selling on Atlas doesn't cannibalize the main channel.
- **A legitimate context:** A "Display Item, Grade B, –40%" listing on a curated outlet marketplace protects brand perception far better than a WhatsApp fire sale.
- **Controlled visibility (future):** Sellers may choose to list under a storefront name rather than their retail brand name, while Atlas still verifies and displays the business identity to buyers after purchase.
- **Recovery-rate framing:** Atlas reports to each seller: "You recovered X% of retail value, versus ~Y% through liquidators." The pitch to sellers is measurable money, not vague reach.
- **Effortless listing:** Photo-first mobile listing flow, bulk upload for chains, and (later) integrations with retail/ERP systems. A store employee should be able to list a display item in under 3 minutes.

## Why Buyers Come Back Daily

An outlet marketplace lives or dies on repeat visits. The mechanics:

- **Uniqueness & urgency:** Most listings are 1-unit. Sold means gone forever. Browsing daily is rewarded.
- **New arrivals feed:** A fresh, curated "Today on Atlas" feed — the daily treasure hunt.
- **Follow & alert:** Follow a brand, category, or search ("Dyson", "espresso machines under €300") and get notified the moment a matching item drops.
- **Drops & collections (later):** Curated themed drops ("Showroom clear-out: Italian furniture", "Open-box January") create appointment shopping.
- **Honest social proof:** Views and "X people following this item" — real scarcity signals, never fake countdown timers or fake discounts. Fake urgency would destroy the trust engine.

## Focus & Rollout Strategy

"Europe's leading outlet marketplace" is the destination, not the starting point. Marketplaces die from being too broad too early.

### Beachhead
- **Launch market: Turkey.** A large retail market where display/open-box inventory is abundant, currently liquidated through exactly the fragmented channels we replace (Instagram, WhatsApp groups, outlet corners), and no curated premium outlet player exists.
- **2–3 categories** where average order value is high: small appliances & electronics, furniture & lighting, home & kitchen premium brands.
- **Supply first:** Recruit 50–100 quality sellers by hand before spending anything on buyer acquisition. An outlet marketplace with empty shelves is dead on arrival; great inventory markets itself.
- Founder-led seller onboarding: we photograph, grade, and list the first sellers' inventory *for them* if needed. Learning their workflow is the product research.

### Expansion (only after liquidity)
1. More categories in Turkey.
2. Adjacent European markets (one at a time).
3. Logistics & value-added services layer.

### What We Measure (North Star)
- **Sell-through rate:** % of listings sold within 30 days. This is the health of the marketplace.
- **Recovery rate:** average % of retail value sellers recover. This is the seller pitch.
- **Grade accuracy:** % of orders where the buyer confirms the product matched its grade. This is the trust engine's KPI.
- Repeat purchase rate and weekly active browsers.

## Revenue Model

Ordered by phase — earn trust first, monetize depth later:

1. **Seller commission** (core, from day one) — success-based, aligned incentives.
2. **Featured listings & sponsored collections** — visibility within the curated experience, clearly labeled.
3. **Premium seller subscription** — bulk tools, analytics, integrations, priority support.
4. **Value-added services (later)** — professional photography/grading service, logistics, "Atlas Verified" physical inspection for high-value items.

We do NOT monetize by loosening curation. Listing fees that pressure us to accept junk are off the table.

## Moat (Why This Is Defensible)

- **Trust brand:** "Atlas Grade B" becoming a term buyers rely on is a brand asset competitors can't copy quickly.
- **Curation discipline:** Big marketplaces structurally cannot curate — their model is infinite selection. Our constraint is our differentiation.
- **Seller relationships & tooling:** Once a retail chain's outlet workflow runs through Atlas (grading, listing, recovery reporting), switching costs are real.
- **Data:** Condition-and-grade price elasticity data (what discount sells a Grade B display TV in 7 days?) makes Atlas's pricing guidance smarter every month.
